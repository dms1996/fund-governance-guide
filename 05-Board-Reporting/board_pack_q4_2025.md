### Market Conditions — Q4 2025

Global equity markets experienced a strong quarter, supported by easing monetary policy from major central banks. The S&P 500 gained 4.8% while the MSCI Europe returned 3.2%. Fixed income markets benefited from the ECB's continued rate-cutting cycle, with European investment-grade credit spreads tightening by approximately 15bps. Emerging markets faced headwinds from a strengthening US dollar in November but recovered in December on improved sentiment around China policy stimulus.

### Performance Commentary

**Global Equity Fund** — The fund outperformed its benchmark by 93bps over the year. Stock selection in US technology and European industrials was the primary driver. The underweight in energy detracted modestly in Q4. The Investment Manager notes that portfolio concentration remains within policy limits at 28.3% in the top 10 holdings.

**European Bond Fund** — Outperformance of 43bps was driven by an overweight in BBB-rated corporate bonds and active duration management during the ECB rate cuts. Credit quality of the portfolio remains high with 94% investment grade.

**Multi-Asset Growth Fund** — The fund's dynamic allocation between equity and fixed income added value, with the equity allocation averaging 63% during the quarter. Alternative asset allocation (5.2%) provided additional diversification.

**Emerging Markets Fund** — Outperformance was driven by overweight positions in India and selective exposure to Latin American commodity exporters. The fund navigated China-related volatility through a tactical underweight maintained since Q2.

**Real Estate Opportunities Fund** — The fund underperformed its benchmark by 73bps. The European commercial real estate market continued to face headwinds from elevated vacancy rates. The Board notes the Investment Manager's plan to increase allocation to logistics assets in 2026.

**Private Credit Fund** — Strong performance driven by direct lending allocations yielding an average spread of Euribor + 425bps. No credit defaults were recorded during the quarter. Vintage diversification remains healthy.

## 8. Regulatory Update

### Central Bank of Ireland (CBI)

1. **UCITS Liquidity Risk Management Guidance (December 2025):** The CBI published updated guidance on liquidity stress testing for UCITS funds, effective 1 July 2026. The Compliance team is conducting a gap analysis against current practices. Preliminary assessment suggests minor enhancements required to stress testing frequency and reporting. A detailed implementation plan will be presented at the Q1 2026 board meeting.

2. **CBI Annual Supervisory Engagement:** The CBI's annual supervisory review letter was received on 5 December 2025. No material findings. Two minor observations related to (i) timeliness of substance reporting and (ii) documentation of outsourcing oversight. Responses have been drafted and are recommended for Board approval (see Appendix B).

3. **PRIIPs KID Review:** Updated PRIIPs KID templates incorporating ESMA's latest technical standards have been prepared for all funds. Effective date: 1 January 2026. All KIDs were published on time.

### European Regulation

4. **AIFMD II Transposition:** Member states are progressing transposition of AIFMD II. Key changes impacting loan-originating AIFs (relevant to Private Credit Fund) are expected to take effect in Q2 2026. Legal counsel is reviewing implications.

5. **DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act):** The platform's DORA compliance programme is on track for the January 2025 application date. ICT third-party risk register has been completed and shared with the CBI.
