## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This document establishes the compliance monitoring framework for Capital Management Co. ("CMC"), a regulated fund management company responsible for the governance and oversight of UCITS and Alternative Investment Funds ("AIFs") domiciled in Ireland and Luxembourg.

The framework ensures that all funds under management operate within applicable regulatory requirements, prospectus restrictions, and internal policies at all times.

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to:

- All UCITS funds managed by CMC
- All Alternative Investment Funds managed under AIFMD authorisation
- All employees, officers, and designated persons of the management company
- Third-party delegates performing regulated activities on behalf of CMC

## 2. Regulatory Framework

### 2.1 UCITS Regulations

CMC UCITS funds are governed by the following principal regulations:

- **EU Directive 2009/65/EC** (UCITS IV Directive) as amended by UCITS V (2014/91/EU)
- **Central Bank (Supervision and Enforcement) Act 2013 (Section 48(1)) (Undertakings for Collective Investment in Transferable Securities) Regulations 2019** (CBI UCITS Regulations)
- **CSSF Regulation 10-4** and related circulars for Luxembourg-domiciled UCITS
- **ESMA Guidelines** on liquidity stress testing, performance fees, and fund naming conventions

Key UCITS investment restrictions monitored include:

- **5/10/40 Rule:** No more than 10% of NAV in securities of a single issuer; aggregate of positions exceeding 5% must not exceed 40% of NAV
- **Counterparty Limits:** Maximum 5% OTC counterparty exposure (10% for credit institutions)
- **Borrowing:** Temporary borrowing limited to 10% of NAV
- **Concentration:** No more than 20% of NAV in a single collective investment scheme
- **Eligible Assets:** All investments must qualify as eligible assets under UCITS regulations

### 2.2 AIFMD Regulations

CMC alternative investment funds are governed by:

- **EU Directive 2011/61/EU** (AIFMD) and associated Level 2 regulation (EU 231/2013)
- **CSSF Circular 18/698** and related Luxembourg AIFM requirements
- **ESMA Guidelines** on reporting obligations under Articles 3(3)(d) and 24(1), (2), and (4)

Key AIFMD requirements monitored include:

- **Leverage Limits:** As disclosed to investors and regulators, monitored via both commitment and gross methods
- **Liquidity Management:** Alignment of investment strategy with redemption terms
- **Annex IV Reporting:** Quarterly or semi-annual reporting to national competent authorities
- **Remuneration Policy:** Compliance with AIFMD remuneration requirements for identified staff

### 2.3 Central Bank of Ireland Requirements

- **Fitness and Probity Regime:** Ongoing compliance for all Pre-Approval Controlled Functions (PCF) and Controlled Functions (CF)
- **Fund Management Company Guidance:** CP86 organisational effectiveness requirements
- **PRISM supervisory engagement** obligations and data submissions
- **Individual Accountability Framework** requirements effective from 2024

## 3. Compliance Monitoring Programme

### 3.1 Overview

The Compliance Monitoring Programme ("CMP") is a risk-based programme of activities designed to verify adherence to all applicable regulatory requirements. The CMP is reviewed and approved by the Board annually.

### 3.2 Monitoring Activities

| Activity | Frequency | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| Investment restriction monitoring | Daily | Compliance Officer |
| NAV oversight and verification | Daily | Risk & Operations |
| Counterparty exposure monitoring | Daily | Compliance Officer |
| Derivative usage review | Daily | Compliance Officer |
| Leverage calculation (AIFs) | Weekly | Risk & Operations |
| AML/KYC file reviews | Monthly | MLRO |
| Prospectus restriction testing | Monthly | Compliance Officer |
| Regulatory filing calendar review | Monthly | Head of Compliance |
| Board reporting pack preparation | Quarterly | Head of Compliance |
| Compliance risk assessment update | Semi-annually | Head of Compliance |
| Full CMP review and update | Annually | Board of Directors |

### 3.3 Pre-Trade and Post-Trade Monitoring

**Pre-Trade Compliance:** All investment orders are screened against regulatory and prospectus restrictions prior to execution. The portfolio management system is configured with hard and soft limits to prevent and flag potential breaches.

**Post-Trade Compliance:** Daily post-trade compliance checks verify that executed trades have not caused any limit breaches. Results are documented and any exceptions are escalated immediately.

### 3.4 Compliance Monitoring Tools

CMC utilises the following systems for compliance monitoring:

- Portfolio management system with integrated pre-trade and post-trade compliance engine
- Independent compliance monitoring platform for daily limit checking
- Automated regulatory reporting tool for CBI and CSSF submissions
- AML/KYC case management system with screening integration

## 4. Investment Restriction Monitoring

### 4.1 UCITS Investment Restrictions

The following restrictions are monitored on a daily basis for all UCITS funds:

**Issuer Concentration (Article 52):**
- Single issuer limit: maximum 10% of NAV
- Aggregate of >5% positions: maximum 40% of NAV
- Government/public issuer exemption: up to 35% per issuer (100% with six-issue diversification)

**Counterparty Exposure:**
- OTC derivative counterparty: maximum 5% of NAV (10% for EU credit institutions)
- Netting agreements and collateral considered in exposure calculation

**Eligible Assets:**
- All holdings verified against UCITS eligible asset criteria
- Transferable securities must be listed or traded on a regulated market
- Maximum 10% in unlisted transferable securities

**Borrowing:**
- Temporary borrowing only, maximum 10% of NAV
- No lending of fund assets except through recognised securities lending programmes

### 4.2 AIFMD Investment Restrictions

**Leverage Monitoring:**
- Gross method and commitment method calculated at minimum weekly
- Internal limits set with appropriate buffers below regulatory maximums
- Real Estate Opportunities Fund: 300% gross / 200% commitment
- Private Credit Fund: 300% gross / 200% commitment

**Prospectus Restrictions:**
- All fund-specific investment restrictions per offering documentation monitored at least monthly
- Any amendments to prospectus restrictions reflected in monitoring system within 5 business days of approval

### 4.3 Escalation Procedures for Investment Breaches

| Severity | Response Time | Escalation |
|---|---|---|
| Active breach (trade-related) | Immediate | Compliance Officer, Investment Manager, Head of Compliance |
| Passive breach (market movement) | Same business day | Compliance Officer, Investment Manager |
| Technical/operational breach | Within 4 hours | Compliance Officer, Operations Manager |

All breaches must be remediated within the timeframes specified in the relevant regulation (typically 3-5 business days for passive UCITS breaches). Active breaches require immediate remediation.

## 5. AML/CTF Framework

### 5.1 Overview

CMC maintains a comprehensive Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing ("AML/CTF") framework in accordance with:

- **Criminal Justice (Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing) Act 2010** (as amended)
- **EU 4th and 5th Anti-Money Laundering Directives** (2015/849 and 2018/843)
- **Central Bank of Ireland AML/CFT Guidelines for the Financial Sector**

### 5.2 Customer Due Diligence

**Standard Due Diligence (SDD):**
All investors are subject to identity verification, beneficial ownership identification, and purpose of investment assessment prior to acceptance.

**Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD):**
EDD is applied in the following circumstances:
- Investors from high-risk jurisdictions (per EU high-risk third country list and internal risk assessment)
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their associates
- Complex or unusual ownership structures
- Transactions with no apparent economic rationale
- Any investor assessed as high AML risk

### 5.3 Ongoing Monitoring

- Investor screening against sanctions lists (EU, UN, OFAC) performed at onboarding and on an ongoing automated basis
- PEP screening at onboarding and refreshed annually
- Transaction monitoring for unusual patterns
- KYC file reviews conducted on a risk-based schedule:
  - High-risk investors: annually
  - Medium-risk investors: every two years
  - Low-risk investors: every three years

### 5.4 Suspicious Transaction Reporting

The Money Laundering Reporting Officer ("MLRO") is responsible for:
- Receiving and assessing internal suspicious activity reports
- Filing Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) with the Financial Intelligence Unit of Ireland
- Maintaining STR records in a confidential register
- Reporting to the Board on AML/CTF matters quarterly

## 6. Breach Management Procedures

### 6.1 Breach Identification

Breaches may be identified through:
- Automated compliance monitoring systems
- Manual compliance checks and reviews
- Self-reporting by staff members
- External audit or regulatory examination findings
- Investor complaints or queries

### 6.2 Breach Classification

| Severity | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Minor/technical breach with no investor impact; self-correcting or easily remediated | Passive UCITS limit breach from market movement; minor data entry error in regulatory filing |
| Medium | Breach requiring active intervention; potential regulatory notification required | Late regulatory filing; temporary leverage breach; reporting error |
| High | Material breach with potential investor detriment or significant regulatory consequence | Active investment limit breach; AML/CTF reporting failure; material misstatement |
| Critical | Severe breach requiring immediate board notification and regulatory disclosure | Systemic compliance failure; fraud; material investor loss |

### 6.3 Breach Response Process

1. **Detection and Initial Assessment:** Compliance Officer assesses the breach within 4 hours of detection
2. **Containment:** Immediate steps taken to prevent further impact
3. **Root Cause Analysis:** Investigation to determine underlying cause
4. **Remediation:** Corrective action implemented and verified
5. **Regulatory Notification:** Where required, notify relevant competent authority within prescribed timeframes
6. **Board Reporting:** All breaches reported to the Board at the next scheduled meeting; High and Critical breaches reported immediately
7. **Lessons Learned:** Review and update of controls to prevent recurrence

### 6.4 Breach Register

All compliance breaches are recorded in the Regulatory Breaches Log, which captures:
- Date of detection and description
- Regulatory provision breached
- Severity classification
- Root cause analysis
- Remediation actions taken
- Regulatory reporting details
- Resolution confirmation and date
- Lessons learned and control enhancements

## 8. Board Reporting

### 8.1 Quarterly Compliance Report

The Head of Compliance presents a quarterly compliance report to the Board covering:

- Summary of compliance monitoring activities performed
- Results of investment restriction monitoring (including any breaches)
- AML/CTF activity summary and MLRO report
- Regulatory breach register update
- Regulatory developments and their impact on CMC funds
- Status of any open regulatory examinations or enquiries
- Compliance risk assessment update
- Upcoming regulatory deadlines

### 8.2 Annual Compliance Report

The annual compliance report includes:

- Assessment of the effectiveness of the compliance monitoring programme
- Summary of all breaches during the year and trend analysis
- Review of compliance resources and capability
- Proposed compliance monitoring programme for the following year
- Regulatory horizon scanning and impact assessment
